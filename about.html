Our Mission

Our mission is to provide Princeton undergraduate students with practical investing experience through the pursuit of market-beating returns.



Our History

Founded in July 2013, Tiger Capital Management is currently the only student-run, privately managed investment fund at Princeton University. Our five founding members envisioned creating a venue for students to share ideas and learn more about investing first-hand. In realizing this vision, our organization strives to offer an unparalleled learning environment and produce the best investment ideas in a rigorous and systematic manner. We continue to believe in our mission and thus seek to enable practical investing education through the pursuit of superior returns.



Our Founding Principles

Intellectual Curiosity: Our aim is to collectively understand investing better.

Partnership: Our organizational structure is flat and meritocratic.

Engagement: Our members are actively involved and passionate about investing.


Our Approach

Investment Objective

Our primary investment objective is to learn about investing. Thus, we believe maintaining a concentrated portfolio enables us to maximize learning and have a deep understanding of all our holdings.

Investment Philosophy

Our investment philosophy is fundamental value-oriented investing. We seek to make investments in companies with large margins of safety.

Investment Process

Our investment process is a rigorous two-stage discussion of the investment idea. We strive to take advantage of our academic background to engage in a research-intensive approach to investing.


Our Focus

Investment Universe

Our investment universe includes equity securitires of small and mid cap companies. Our focus is on US equities, but we also invest opportunistically internationally. Our investment time horizon is 2-3 years.

Investment Sourcing

Our fund sources investment ideas from six Industry Groups led by our Partners: Consumer Products, Global Macro, Industrials & Materials, Healthcare, Energy, and Technology, Media & Telecommunications (TMT).
